## What is Parasite SEO?

Using high-authority platforms (Reddit DA 91, Digg, LinkedIn, etc.) to rank content in search engines without building your own website. You borrow their domain authority to rank instantly.

**The core principle:** Create content on trusted platforms ‚Üí They rank ‚Üí You get free traffic

## The 5-Step Parasite SEO Framework

### Step 1: Choose Your Platform

**Reddit** (DA 91)
- 108M daily active users
- 2B monthly visits
- Subreddit model = your own micro-site
- Best for: Communities, tutorials, long-form content

**Digg** (Rising DA)
- Just relaunched - first-mover advantage
- Less competition than Reddit
- Similar community model
- Best for: Viral content, quick wins

**LinkedIn** (DA 98)
- Best for B2B
- Professional credibility
- Long-form articles perform well
- Best for: Thought leadership, B2B services

### Step 2: Create Your Platform Presence

**For Reddit:**
1. Go to Reddit ‚Üí Create Community
2. Name it around your OFFER (not just interest)
   - Examples: "AI SEO Insider", "E-commerce Growth Hacks", "Local Business AI"
   - Ask ChatGPT: "Give me 5 subreddit names for [your offer]"
3. Add banner, topics, description
4. Setup takes ~60 seconds

**For Digg:**
1. Create Digg account
2. Follow similar naming strategy
3. Start engaging before posting

**Naming Best Practices:**
- Name around what you SELL
- Make it brandable and searchable
- Check availability first
- Example: "AI SEO Insider" for AI+SEO services

### Step 3: Content Engine (The Core)

**Source Material:**
- YouTube video transcripts (already have content)
- Blog posts
- Social media posts
- Customer questions
- Case studies

**AI Content Generation (Claude):**
1. Take existing content/transcript
2. Paste into Claude
3. Use custom prompt to generate Reddit-formatted article
4. Claude writes in your voice, on any topic
5. Never gets writer's block

**Key Insight:** You're not a writer anymore - you're a creative director. AI is your writing team. You edit, not write.

### Step 4: Quality Control (CRITICAL)

**Why Quality Control Matters:**
- AI hallucinates and makes things up
- Can say controversial things
- Must be customer-facing quality
- Your reputation is at stake

**Quality Control Checklist:**
- [ ] Read entire article before posting
- [ ] Check for factual errors
- [ ] Remove hallucinations
- [ ] Ensure brand consistency
- [ ] Verify links work
- [ ] Check formatting
- [ ] Test on mobile

**Pro Tip:** Don't fully auto-post. Quality control = 80% of success.

### Step 5: Publish & Optimize

**Post Structure:**
1. **Headline** - 80% of effort (80/20 rule)
2. **Content** - Repurposed from existing material
3. **Embed YouTube video** - Retargeting + trust
4. **Funnel links** - Lead magnets, sales pages, free resources
5. **Call to action** - What do you want them to do?

**Analytics to Track:**
- View counts per post
- Traffic sources
- Engagement (upvotes, comments, shares)
- Click-through rates on links
- Ranked keywords

**Headline Tips:**

- Test multiple variations
- Make it benefit-driven
- Use numbers and specifics
- Create curiosity gaps

**Example Headlines:**

- "The 24-Hour SEO Strategy That Beats 6 Months of Traditional SEO"
- "AI Automation That Generated 1,300 Leads This Week"

Every post should include:

2. **Lead magnet** - Capture email
3. **Free resource** - Value first

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: Spammy Content
- Don't just promote
- Provide genuine value
- Reddit community detects spam instantly

### Mistake #2: No Quality Control
- Never post AI content raw
- Always review before publishing
- Hallucinations destroy credibility

### Mistake #3: Wrong Niche Name
- Don't name it around your interest
- Name it around what you SELL
- Example: "SEO Services" not "I Love Keywords"

### Mistake #4: Inconsistency
- Post daily minimum
- 30-day challenge recommended
- Compounding takes time

### Mistake #5: Ignoring Analytics
- Check view counts
- Test different headlines
- Optimize based on data
